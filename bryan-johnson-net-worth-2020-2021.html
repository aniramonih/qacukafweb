Career

Johnson launched three startups, while at university, between 1999 and 2003. The first startup sold cell phones, which helped him to pay his way through Brigham Young University. Furthermore, he hired other college students to sell service plans along with cell phones in the same business.  Consequently, Johnson earned about a $300 commission on each sale.

Moreover, Johnson started two other businesses, Inquist, and Braintree. Johnson co-founded Inquist with three different partners, with combined features of Vonage and Skype. The company was dissolved in 2001, after which he joined his brother and another partner on a $70 million real estate project. However, the project did not yield set sales goals.

Johnson, while working at a part-time job selling credit card processing services to businesses, formed the idea for Braintree. The firm increased due to the clients in the technology industry, including OpenTable, Uber, Shopify, Airbnb, among others. The company became the 47th on Inc. magazine’s 2011 list of the 500 fastest-growing companies. Later, Braintree purchased Venmo, a startup that lets users send and receive money from each other electronically.

Johnson announced the creation of OS Fund, which he backed with $100 million of his capital In October 2014. The fund invests in early-stage science and technology companies led by inventors and scientists who aim to help humanity through quantum leap discoveries at the operating system level. He launched an online video series, “Explorations,” In 2016, which featured interviews with leaders of some of the companies backed by OS Fund

In 2016, Johnson started Kernel, making a personal investment of $100 million. The company’s goal was to build an implantable device to improve brain function in humans while interfacing with artificial intelligence (AI).
