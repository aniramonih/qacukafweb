Patton Oswalt Full Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Education

Patton Oswalt, born on January 27, 1969, in Portsmouth, Virginia, had a childhood shaped by his parents, Carla and Larry J. Oswalt. His father’s career in the Marine Corps instilled a sense of discipline and adaptability in him.

Oswalt’s educational journey took him to the College of William & Mary, where he majored in English, a choice that undoubtedly honed his skills in storytelling and writing.

Rise to Fame

Oswalt’s foray into comedy began in 1988, marking the start of a journey that would see him become a household name.

His unique blend of observational humor and a knack for voice acting led to his first major break in the sitcom The King of Queens, where he played the lovable Spence Olchin.

Voice Acting and Film Career

Perhaps one of Oswalt’s most notable contributions to the entertainment industry is his voice role as Remy in the Pixar film Ratatouille. This role, coupled with his voice work in The Secret Life of Pets 2, highlights his versatility as an actor.

His filmography is extensive, with appearances in movies like Zoolander, Blade: Trinity, and The Circle, showcasing his ability to adapt to various genres.

Television Appearances and Stand-Up Comedy

Oswalt’s television career is as varied as it is impressive. From narrating The Goldbergs to his recurring role in Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D., he has demonstrated a range that few can match.

His stand-up comedy, characterized by its sharp wit and relatable content, has earned him an Emmy and a Grammy, solidifying his status as a comedy icon.

FAQs about Patton Oswalt

Who is Patton Oswalt?

He is an American stand-up comedian and actor known for his roles in sitcoms like The King of Queens and voicing Remy in the Pixar film Ratatouille.

When and where was he born?

Oswalt was born on January 27, 1969, in Portsmouth, Virginia. He was named after General George S. Patton.

What is his educational background?

He graduated from the College of William & Mary in 1991 with a Bachelor of Arts in English and was made an honorary Doctor of Arts by the college in May 2023.

How did he start his career in comedy?

Oswalt began performing stand-up comedy on July 18, 1988, and after writing for MADtv, he gained recognition with his own comedy special for HBO in 1996.

What are some of Oswalt’s most notable roles?

Beyond his role in The King of Queens, Oswalt voiced Remy in Ratatouille, appeared in films like Magnolia, and 22 Jump Street, and had numerous television appearances including Seinfeld and Parks and Recreation.
