Early Life of Michael Wilbon

Michael Ray Wilbon was born on November 19th, 1958, in Chicago, Illinois. He was raised on the South Side by working-class parents. Wilbon attended St. Ignatius College Preparatory School, graduating in 1976. He later enrolled at the Medill School of Journalism at Northwestern University, earning a journalism degree in 1980. During his college years, he wrote for the school paper, “The Daily Northwestern,” and eventually became a trustee of Northwestern University.

Career of Michael Wilbon

Michael Wilbon began his newspaper career in 1980 with “The Washington Post,” where he covered various sports stories, including Major League Baseball, the National Football League, and the National Basketball Association.
