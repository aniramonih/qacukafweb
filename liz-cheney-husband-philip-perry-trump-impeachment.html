But as well as sharing their personal lives together, politician Liz Cheney and her lawyer husband Philip Perry both have interests in trying to create positive change through their work.

Who is Liz Cheney's husband Philip Perry?

Perry, 58, was born on October 16, 1964.

He is known as an American attorney and was a political appointee in the administration of George W. Bush.

Before his career, Perry was a student at Colorado College, where he pursued a BA degree in English before attending Cornell Law School in 1990, where he earned his Juris Doctor degree.

Perry got his start in politics in 1997 while serving as counsel to the US Senate during the 1996 presidential campaign finance abuse case.

Over the years, he has also been the acting associate attorney general at the Department of Justice, general counsel of the Office of Management and Budget, and general counsel of the Department of Homeland Security.

He is best known for "his pioneering work litigating biotechnology issues and his work on constitutional and federal regulatory maters," and was later given the title “Litigation Trailblazer” by National Law Journal in 2018.

As of 2022, Perry works as a partner at the law firm Latham and Watkins LLP.

Policymaker Liz and her attorney husband have become a Washington, DC power couple, standing out as common-sense Republicans amid the chaos that followed the January 6, 2021, Capitol riots.

What did Liz Cheney say about Donald Trump ally Steve Bannon?

On October 20, 2021, Cheney made headlines after she blasted former Donald Trump ally, Steve Bannon, during a committee hearing on the January 2021, Capitol riots.

Bannon is known as the former White House's chief strategist, and has publicly been refusing to cooperate with the House committee's investigation because of his "executive privilege," despite being served with a subpoenas by the committee.

The night prior, Cheney blasted Bannon saying that not only is his privilege invalid, but Trump's refusal to cooperate insinuates that he was "personally involved" in the planning and execution of the riot.

Cheney said: "Mr Bannon's and Mr Trump's privilege arguments do appear to reveal one thing, however: they suggest that President Trump was personally involved in the planning and execution of January 6th.

"And we will get to the bottom of that."

Democratic Rep Bennie Thompson of Mississippi, another member of the January 6 committee, later expanded on Cheney's comments to CNN adding that he thinks the term "plan" is relative.

He told the media outlet: "There's no question he tweeted about it in the very beginning.

"The majority of the people in this country didn't know what January 6 was all about when he did it.

"He created the narrative up to the point. Planned the rally -- and obviously he said, you need to go to the Hill and let people know you don't like what's going on.

"So, people left the rally that he had invited them to and they came to the Hill.

"And the rest we saw in our own eyes."

Do Liz and Philip have any children?

Liz and Phil married in 1993.

Throughout the course of their marriage, the couple have welcomed five children together.

Their children are Elizabeth, Kate, Richard, Grace and Philip Jr.
