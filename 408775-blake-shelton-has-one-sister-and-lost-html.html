Blake Shelton shares an unbreakable bond with his sister that endures to this day, even after their brother's heartbreaking death. Their sibling's loss has shaped the singer, his sister, and their family.

Country music superstar Blake Shelton recognizes the contribution of his beloved family, including his sole surviving sister, to his remarkable success in the music industry.

Blake Shelton's sister has been with him through thick and thin, even during the darkest hours when tragedy struck their family and changed their lives forever.

Blake Shelton & His Sister Share a Deep Bond after Their Sibling's Death

Blake Shelton had two siblings: sister Endy Intieri Shelton and brother Richie Shelton. Sadly, Richie passed away over three decades ago, leaving Blake with his sister Endy. Endy shared a nostalgic post on Instagram in 2020. Her post read:

"Happy siblings day to me and my brothers #Blakeshelton and late #Ritchie."

Even after Richie's untimely death, the renowned country musician and Endy have managed to maintain a deep connection, despite their separate obligations and families.

She is Blake's number one supporter, as she helps promote his career by adding his website to her Instagram bio. Endy's love and support for Blake extend to his family. She has developed a close relationship with Blake's new wife, pop singer Gwen Stefani.

Blake admitted thinking about Richie daily and that one never truly gets over such a loss.

One of the many unforgettable moments she and her family shared with Blake's family was in 2016. At Blake's Oklahoma ranch, they all enjoyed a restful vacation and a lively dance party at home with other members of their extended families.

Blake Shelton Was 14 When His Brother Died

Blake was just 14 years old when his brother Richie, who was 24 then, tragically died in a car crash in 1990. During the incident, Richie was in a car with his girlfriend, who was 20 years old, and her son. This event which left no survivors, changed Blake and his family, leaving them devastated.

In 2017, the singer took to Twitter and paid tribute to his brother's death anniversary. He realized that the experience made him understand the value of life and the importance of seizing the present moment.

The following year, he reflected on Richie's death again. Blake admitted thinking about Richie daily and that one never truly gets over such a loss. He described his brother as his role model and explained how his absence created a void in his life.

The tragic event inspired him to collaborate with his ex-wife, singer Miranda Lambert, on the song "Over You." Despite his brother's absence, the singer credits his extended family for shaping him into the person he is today.

He said he grew up with hardworking individuals who knew how to have a good time. Blake described his family as natural entertainers and attributes his sense of humor and talent to them.

Despite their deep sorrow over losing their brother, Blake, his sister, and their families find the strength to keep moving forward. Aside from music, Blake keeps himself busy with other projects and shows. He is best known for his judging stint in "The Voice" with Kelly Clarkson, Niall Horan, and Chance the Rapper.
