Career

After completing his high school, Nolan Ryan entered the 1965 MLB draft, where the New York Mets selected him. He was assigned to the Appalachian League for Marion Mets. Due to illness, he missed the 1967 season.

In the 1968 season, Ryan was more of a sports starter and reliever. In 1969, he was a part of the winning team of the World Series. He was later traded to the California Angels for Jim Fregosi. With the Angles, he was allowed to pitch.

He struck out three batters on nine pitches against the Boston Red Sox in 1972. The following year, he made a struck out of 383 batters. In the same year, he also threw two no-hitters. In 1974, he pitched at 100.8 miles/hour.

Ryan remained with the California Angels till 1979. During this time, he also led the American League. Following the 1979 season, he became a free agent. In 1980, he joined the Houston Astros. His fifth no-hitter came in 1981, and that same year he won the title of National League ERA.

He was with the Astros till 1988. From 1989 to 1993, Ryan played for the Texas Rangers. He completed his career by playing in 27 seasons of Major League Baseball. In his career, he has thrown a total of seven no-hitters.

Awards & Achievements
