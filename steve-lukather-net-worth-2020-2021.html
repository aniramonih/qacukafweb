Early Life & Biography

Steve Lukather was born on 21 October 1957, in the city of San Fernando Valley, California. He got the influence playing guitar and keyboard at an early age of seven. His father bought him a Kay acoustic guitar when he was seven only, and he started copying the playing style of the Beatles.

Their album “changed his life” was the most significant influence of his childhood, and George Harrison became his idol. He completed his schooling from Grant High School and met his future band partners David Paich and the Porcaro brothers, with whom he later founded their band “Toto.”

Career

Steve Lukather is the co-founder of the rock band Toto, which he formed along with his school mates David Paich and the Porcaro brothers. Steve was the lead guitarist of the group. Lukather grabbed three Grammy Awards playing for the band, twice as an artist and once as a producer.

Jeff Porcaro was also the well-deserved leader of the group, who influenced him a lot. After Jeff died in 1992, the band was playing without a lead vocalist. Several years passed, but they were unable to find the perfect one. Steve took charge of the vocals and performed most of the tracks like 1992’s Kingdom of Desire and 1995’s Tambu.

Steve was considered one of the most notable guitarists of the 1970s era, after performing in many stage shows of Los Angeles. Also named as the Top 10 guitarist of all time by Gibson Guitar Corporation, which is a well-known guitar manufacturing company.

Some of Steve’s popular solo tracks are “Back on the Road” and “You Went Away.” He also performed with famous singers of the generation like Lionel Richie and Michael Jackson. Some of his hit solo albums are Lukather (1989), Candyman (1994), and Transition (2013).

Awards & Achievements

Considered as one of the greatest guitarists of all time, Steve Lukather has won several awards in his career, both playing for his band Toto, and individually. Some of his major achievements are listed below:

  • Grammy Award for Best R&B Song(1982)
  • Grammy Award for Producer of the Year: Toto (1982)
  • Grammy Award for Album of the Year: Toto – Toto IV (1982)
  • Grammy Award for Record of the Year: Toto – “Rosanna” (1982)
  • Grammy Award for Best Pop Instrumental Album(2002)
  • Musicians Hall Of Fame (2009)
  • Guitar Player Magazine – Lifetime Achievement Award (2015)
