Early Life and Career of Dick Wolf

Born on December 20, 1946, in New York City, Dick Wolf’s journey to becoming a television mogul began in the heart of the Big Apple. His mother, an Irish Catholic homemaker, and his father, a Jewish advertising executive, provided a diverse cultural background for Wolf.

He attended prestigious schools including Saint David’s School, The Gunnery, and Phillips Academy. Wolf later graduated from the University of Pennsylvania in 1969, where he was a member of the Zeta Psi fraternity.

Wolf’s career in the entertainment industry didn’t start in television. In his early twenties, he worked as a copywriter for the advertising firm Benton & Bowles, where he created successful slogans for brands like Crest toothpaste and National Airlines.

However, his passion for the film industry was undeniable. He spent his spare time writing screenplays, even collaborating with Oliver Stone, who was then a struggling screenwriter.

In 1977, Wolf moved to Los Angeles, the city that would become the launchpad for his illustrious career in television. He had three screenplays produced, including “Masquerade” starring Rob Lowe and Meg Tilly.

His talent for storytelling was recognized, and he landed a job as a staff writer on the police procedural “Hill Street Blues.” This was the beginning of his remarkable journey in the world of television, a journey that would eventually lead to the creation of some of the most successful TV franchises and a net worth of $550 million.

Overview of the Law & Order Series

The show’s unique format, which divides each episode into two parts – the investigation of a crime and the prosecution of the defendant – was groundbreaking and has been replicated in various spin-offs.
