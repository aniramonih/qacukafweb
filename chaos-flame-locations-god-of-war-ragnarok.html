While Chaos Flames are crafting resource, they are separate from most other resources in God of War Ragnarok. While most resources can be found in chests, this is not the case with Chaos Flames. To claim them, God of War Ragnarok players must earn them by defeating the bosses that are part of the main story. There are five Chaos Flames in total that are obtained through the main story. Since these bosses are not optional, players won’t have to worry about missing out on potential upgrades.

Where to Get Chaos Flame God of War ?

Fortunately, there are many Chaos Flames to be found as you progress through the story. Allowing Kratos to naturally upgrade his power level over the course of the game, even if you don’t seek the beaten path. However, those looking to maximize their power will want to take on alternative challenges. Kratos’s blades of Chaos is a major source of his power to defeating the enemy. We give you location of each Chaos flame at below.

1. Chaos Flame (The Forge Dreki)

The first upgrade you can get for the Blade is part of the story – one you’ll find in the first main realm of Svartalfheim in your adventure. Once you reach The Forge favor on your way to the Pitmines in The Quest for Tyr, you’ll be forced into a confrontation with this massive beast. Beat it to claim the Chaos Flame.

2. Chaos Flame (Freyr’s Camp Forest Ancient)

The next upgrade you can find is also story-based, but you won’t find it until much later – the area of ​​Vanaheim, to be exact. In Chapter 6 – The Reckoning, you’ll visit the lush forests of Vanaheim, and engage in battle with an enemy you may recognize from the previous game – an ancient Soul Eater. Defeat it when its core opens and you’ll earn another Chaos Flame.

3. Chaos Flame (Well of Urd Frost Phantom)

Another story upgrade can be found during Chapter 9 – Word of Fate, as you venture back into Midgard and locate the mysterious Well of Urd. During this journey you will face off against a boss known as the Frost Phantom, and must stun it before it destroys its Runic Springs. Do this, and you’ll get not only a frozen flame, but a jewel of Yggdrasil to boot.

4. Chaos Flame (Muspelheim Crucible Trials)

While technically one of the first Chaos Flames you can get that isn’t part of the main story, you can try it as soon as you finish Chapter 10 – Forging Destiny. You’ll need to find both Muspelheim Seed halves, and then travel to the realm to take the 6 optional Crucible Trials. Complete them all, and a special Nornir chest will be unlocked that holds the Chaos Flame for upgrading the Blades of chaos.

5. Chaos Flame (Freyr’s Camp Twin Dreki)

Back on the main story path, you’ll return to Vanaheim during Chapter 13 – Creatures of Prophecy. Along the way, you’ll travel back to Friar’s camp through a familiar ruin that extends to the river delta, only this time not one but two drakes will ambush you here. Defeat them both and the Chaos Flame will be yours.
