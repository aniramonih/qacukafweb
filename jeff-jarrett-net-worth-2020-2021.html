Early Life & Biography

Jeff Leonard Jarrett, popularly known as Jeff Jarrett, was born in Hendersonville, Tennessee, in July 1967. His father Jerry Jarrett and Tojo Yamamoto trained him to be a wrestler. He worked for his father’s Continental Wrestling Association as a referee in March 1986. Jarrett made his in-ring debut at the age of 18 on April 6, 1986.

Jarrett is a third-generation wrestler. His father wrestled, his maternal grandfather, Eddie Marlin, wrestled as well, while his paternal grandmother, Christine, was employed by Nashville-based wrestling promotion Gulas/Welch Promotions.

Jarrett’s popularity soared when he debuted in the World Wrestling Federation (WWF, now WWE) in 1992. He wrestled for the World Championship Wrestling from 1996 to 1997 before returning to the WWF from 1997 to 1999.

Personal Life

Jeff Jarrett’s first marriage was to his childhood sweetheart Jill Gregory in 1992. He had three daughters with Gregory – Joslyn, Jaclyn, and Jerlyn. Jill Gregory passed away in 2007 due to cancer. Jeff married wrestler Karen Smedley in 2010.

Career

Jeff Jarrett kicked off his career while working as a referee for his father Jerry Jarrett’s Continental Wrestling Association in 1986. His professional life was shaped by his father’s training. Jeff also trained under professional wrestler late Tojo Yamamoto. Jeff debuted in a match in April 1986. He wrestled for his father’s United States Wrestling Association and on the independent circuit.

Jeff was part of the World Wrestling Federation in 1993 and wrestled there till 1996. He wrestled for World Championship Wrestling from 1996-1997 and returned to the WWF from 1997 to 1999. He re-joined WCW in 1999 but was fired live on air by Vince McMahon in 2001. He wrestled for World Wrestling All-Stars from 2001 to 2003.

Jeff, along with his father, created J Sports and Entertainment and started Total Nonstop Action Wrestling in 2002. In April 2014, Jeff started Global Force Wrestling. Throughout his career, he won several NWA World Heavyweight Championships and four WCW World Heavyweight Championships, among several other major championships.

Awards & Achievements

Jeff Jarrett has had 81 championship reigns throughout his career. He has won the WCW World Heavyweight Champion four times, NWA World Heavyweight Champion six times, and WWF Intercontinental Champion six times.

He has also won the coveted USWA Unified World Heavyweight Championship thrice, WWA World Heavyweight Championship twice, USWA Unified World Heavyweight Championship thrice, and AAA Mega Championship twice.
