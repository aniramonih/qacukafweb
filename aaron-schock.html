Aaron Schock Bio | Wiki

Aaron Schock is a famous former American politician. He worked as a Republican U.S. Representative for Illinois’ 18th congressional district from 2009 until 2015. He was the youngest member of Congress, at age 27. Schock resigned from Congress in March 2015 amid a scandal involving his use of public and campaign funds. In March 2020, he came out as gay despite a voting record of consistently opposing LGBTQ rights.

Aaron Schock Office

He first decided to run for the Peoria Board of Education a few months after graduating from high school. However, he did not get on the ballot because he did not have the required number of valid signatures on his petition to run for office.

Schock ran for a seat in the Illinois House of Representatives when he was 23 years old. He defeated four-term incumbent Democrat Ricca Slone, by just 235 votes out of 40,000 ballots cast. He became the youngest member of the Illinois General Assembly in state history. Moreover, he also defeated Democrat Bill Spears, winning 58 percent of the total vote in the reelection in 2006.

He was the chief sponsor of 38 bills, of which 13 became law that dealt with education, child protection, prescription drug savings, veterans’ assistance, road construction, and high-tech identity theft. In addition, in his second term, Schock worked as Director of Development and Construction for Petersen Companies of Peoria.

Aaron Schock Resignation

Schock resigned from Congress in March 2015 amid a scandal of using public and campaign funds. He resigned in the wake of the congressional ethics investigation into reports that he used taxpayer money to fund lavish trips and events. Schock pleaded not guilty on his charges and the prosecutors reached a deferred prosecution agreement with him in March 2019 where he paid payment of $100,000 in restitution. Moreover, he pleaded guilty to a misdemeanor count of failing to properly report expenses.
