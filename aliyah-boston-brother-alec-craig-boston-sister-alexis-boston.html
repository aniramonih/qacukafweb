Aliyah Boston is a rising star in the world of professional basketball.

Born on December 11, 2001, she has significantly impacted the court as a power forward and centre for the Indiana Fever in the Women’s National Basketball Association (WNBA).

With an impressive skill set and versatility, Boston has proven to be a force to be reckoned with.

She has not only achieved success at the professional level but also shined on the international stage, winning multiple gold medals while representing the United States.

Boston’s talent and dedication were evident throughout her college career, where she decided to enter the 2023 WNBA draft, preceding her remaining eligibility due to the Covid-19 pandemic.

As the first overall pick in the draft, Boston’s future in the WNBA is filled with immense potential and excitement.

Her remarkable collegiate record of 129 wins and nine losses speaks volumes about her impact on the game. Keep an eye on Aliyah Boston as she leaves her mark in the basketball world.

Aliyah Boston Brother Alec Craig Boston

Aliyah Boston, the talented basketball player, has a deeply personal story that fuels her drive and determination.

She tragically lost her brother, Alec Craig Boston, who was born prematurely and did not survive.

This heartbreaking loss has undoubtedly shaped Aliyah’s perspective on life and sports.

Despite enduring such a profound tragedy, she has shown incredible resilience and strength, both on and off the court.

With the support of her loved ones and community, Aliyah has overcome immense pressure and expectations to become a shining star in the basketball world.

Her ability to persevere in adversity is a testament to her character and unwavering spirit.

As Aliyah continues to make her mark in the basketball arena, her brother Alec’s memory is a powerful source of inspiration.

With a bright future ahead, Aliyah Boston’s journey transcends the game and reminds us of the indomitable human spirit.

Aliyah Boston Sister Alexis Boston

Aliyah Boston’s sister, Alexis Boston, is a remarkable individual passionate about sports and the medical field.

As a dedicated athlete, she made her mark on the basketball court, playing two years as a varsity starter for Holy Name Central Catholic under the guidance of coach Barry Finneron.

Alexis also showcased her skills on the AAU basketball stage, competing for the Excel team.

Beyond her athletic pursuits, Alexis pursued her education at the University of South Carolina, graduating in 2021 with a bachelor’s degree in sports and fitness administration/management.

At the university, she served as an equipment manager for the women’s basketball team, further solidifying her connection to the sport.

Alexis’s commitment to giving back is evident through her involvement with Miracle Babies, a foundation initiated by her mother and aunt.

Having experienced the challenges of premature birth firsthand, Alexis and her family raise funds to support needy families by donating to a hospital in their hometown of St. Thomas, USVI.

Alexis’s dedication to community service and her desire to make a positive impact exemplifies the values shared by her and her sister Aliyah.

Aliyah Boston Parents: Cleone Boston and Algernon Boston

Aliyah Boston’s parents, Cleone Boston and Algernon Boston have played an integral role in her life and basketball career.

Cleone, as the president of the Miracle Babies Support Foundation, has been dedicated to supporting the neonatal care unit at Schneider Regional Medical Center.

Alongside her sister, Cleone founded the organization in 2001, demonstrating her commitment to making a difference in the lives of premature infants and their families.

Aliyah’s father, Al, has been a pillar of support throughout her basketball journey.

He and Cleone have been present at every important game she has played in college, showing unwavering support and pride in their daughter’s accomplishments.

Aliyah’s collegiate career at the University of South Carolina has been remarkable, with her team securing two SEC tournament championships, three Final Four appearances, and even clinching the 2022 national title.

Cleone and Algernon’s love, encouragement, and presence in Aliyah’s life have undoubtedly contributed to her success on and off the court.
