The way “you” is spelt in the messages sent to Wriddhiman Saha has led people to believe Boria Majumdar is the journalist who threatened the cricketer 

Having pulled out of this year’s Ranji Trophy after being informed by Chetan Sharma and Rahul Dravid that he was no longer in consideration for the Indian cricket team, Wriddhiman Saha finally sat down to talk about the set of events which had unraveled. He spoke about a slew of topics in his recent interview from his withdrawal from the Ranji Trophy to his talks with the BCCI.

The wicket-keeper who had a mini-period where he managed to play for the national test team went onto talk about the chief selector told him he was no longer in reckoning for a place in the side. Wriddhiman further spoke about how head coach Dravid had advised him to call it quits on his career altogether owing to his age and contemplate retirement.

However, the interview was far from the only thing which made heads turn. Wriddhiman took to Twitter, sharing WhatsApp messages he had received from a so called respected journalist one, where the cricketer is being threatened after refusing to give an interview to the journalist.

While Wriddhiman refrained from naming the journalist, cricket Twitter was quick to get its investigative glasses out to scour for who the person in the WhatsApp chats could be. And one name has finally been nailed down with a magnitude of people believing it to be Boria Majumdar owing to the way you has been spelt.

Boria who sends out tweets spelling you as “ypu” is said to be the journalist losing his marbles after Wriddhiman refused him an interview with the messages seeing you spelt as ypu as well.
