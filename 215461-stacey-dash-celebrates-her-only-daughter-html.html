Stacey Dash Celebrates Her Only Daughter Lola's 17th Birthday with a Sweet Throwback Photo

 Posted on August 26, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  439 words  |  Martina Birk

Stacey Dash penned a short letter on Instagram to her daughter, Lola, as she turned 17: I am so blessed to be your Momma."

Stacey Dash recently celebrated her daughter's 17th birthday with a sweet Instagram post that featured a throwback photo.

A TRIBUTE TO LOLA

The post came with an old photo of the actress kissing her then little girl on the cheek, while Lols smiled beside her mom.

"You are so kind, loving, funny and smart," the proud mom described her daughter. "We've been through so much and you never waver. You are never shaken. Your strength strengthens me."

Dash ended the post with a short prayer, asking the almighty to guide her daughter through this life and "keep her safe."

The "First Lady" actress also has a 28-year-old son, Austin. Dash admitted that her children are her source of strength.

She also shared that among the things she teaches them is to be humble, especially when they make mistakes.

She also recommended channeling a person's energy into the things that make them happy, to draw positivity from it, be it an activity, a person, or anything similar.

DASH'S FOURTH MARRIAGE OVER

Recently, the "Clueless" star announced her separation from her fourth husband, Jeffrey Marty, whom she wed in 2018, roughly ten days after they met.

Dash initially called the cops, accusing her husband of choking her. However, the actress was later arrested for domestic battery, seeing as her husband had red scratch marks on his arm.

Marty bailed his ex-wife out shortly and Florida prosecutors dropped the case months after. Dash was previously wed to Brian Lovell, James Maby, and Emmanuel Xuereb.

Despite her situation, Dash remains positive as she fixes her divorce and keeps sane from the on-going pandemic.

ADVICE TO THE PUBLIC

The actress advised her fans and followers on social media to pinpoint the source of negativity in her life and stay away from it.

She also recommended channeling a person's energy into the things that make them happy, to draw positivity from it, be it an activity, a person, or anything similar.

As she ended her post, Dash said it is essential "to ensure that the world and its problems don't consume you and tear you down."
