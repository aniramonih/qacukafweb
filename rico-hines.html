Career Highlights

  • Rico Hines began his basketball career by playing college ball for the UCLA Bruins. Later on, he’d leave the college with a BA in African-American studies in 2002.
  • Rico finally started his coaching career four years later. He was employed as an athletic development assistant in 2006. This was for the Golden State Warriors under head coach Don Nelson.
  • In 2010, Rico left the Golden State Warriors and moved to St. John’s Red Storm to work as an assistant coach for Steve Lavin. Rico would stay with the team for five years, before departing in 2015. His next opportunity came in 2016 once the Reno Bighorns came calling. This is a team that is based in the NBA’s Development League. They brought Rico on board to serve as the assistant coach.
