Early Life & Biography

Scott Thompson was born in Rockledge, Florida, to Larry Thompson, and Dona Thompson. He is the youngest child of the family, and his father worked as a NASA engineer. He got the nickname Carrot Top from a swimming coach when he was young, and this was in reference to his red hair. Later in his life, the red hair became his trademark.

He studied at Cocoa High School, where he was a drums player in the concert and marching band. After completing high school, he went to Florida Atlantic University in Boca Rato, and that is when he started doing stand-up comedy.

Career

Carrot Top started doing stand-up comedy when he was in his first year at Florida Atlantic University. He has made his comedy exceptional by the use of a prop to enhance his act, and in return, he is among the most celebrated comedians of all times. Some of the props that he uses are his red hair, string telephones, heels with training wheels, and paper cups, and all these help in inducing humor.

By 1992, Scott’s popularity had sky-rocketed, and this earned him a spot as a guest on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno. The show helped him get exposure to a much wider audience and broadened his fan base. After the death of Sam Kinison, who was a legendary comedian in 1992, Carrot top filled in his spot, and this also meant greater recognition.

Some of the shows that he appeared in include Tugger: The Jeep 4×4 Who Wanted to Fly, Space Ghost Coast to Coast, George Lopez, and Larry the Cable Guy’s Christmas Spectacular among others. He got a job as a continuity announcer for Cartoon Network in 1995, and he held the position until 1999. From 1994 to 1996, he was the producer and also starred in the early morning show called Top’s AM Mayhem.

1996 marked the debut appearance of Carrot Top on the big screen, and this was in the film Hourglass. It was followed by a feature film titled Pure Danger, the same year. He had a breakthrough in his career after the movie Chairman of the Board. His greatest breakthrough, though came after he appeared on the commercial for AT & T’s ‘1-800-Call-ATT.

Several viewers demanded his humor, and as a result, he became a permanent feature in shows like Reno 911, Politically Incorrect, and Hollywood Squares, among others. Most recently, he appeared on the Tosh 0 show, where he received a whole-body massage from Daniel Tosh.

Awards & Achievements

The outstanding performances of Carrot Top have not gone unnoticed. In 1994, he received the American Comedy Award for the Funniest Male Stand-up Comic. In addition, he was nominated for the Razzie Award in 1999.
