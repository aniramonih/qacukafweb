Big Sean is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter who has released albums, mixtapes, and tracks including Dark Sky Paradise, Hall of Fame, My Last (ft. Chris Brown), Finally Famous, Detroit, Detroit 2, Marvin & Chardonnay (ft. Kanye West and Roscoe Dash), Beware (ft. Lil Wayne and Jhené Aiko), I Decided, Mercy (with Kanye West, Pusha T, and 2 Chainz), One Man Can Change the World (ft. Kanye West and John Legend), Bounce Back, Paradise, Jump Out the Window, Wolves (ft. Post Malone), and Single Again. He signed with Kanye West’s GOOD Music in 2007, Def Jam Recordings in 2008, and Roc Nation in 2014.

    Big Sean Facts

  • He was born in Santa Monica, California, but grew up in Detroit, Michigan. He was raised by his mother and grandmother.
  • In May 2007, Big Sean signed a deal with the record label GOOD Music (Getting Out Our Dreams), founded by Kanye West in 2004.
  • He had done a role as a don in a short movie titled Cruel Summer, released in 2012.
  • He has appeared on the TV show So You Think You Can Dance as a writer and performer in 2012 and 2013.
  • On August 4, 2011, Sean was arrested due to sexual assault in New York.
  • He released his 5th studio album, Detroit 2, on September 4, 2020, through GOOD Music and Def Jam Recordings. The album features guest appearances from artists like Eminem, Post Malone, Young Thug, Travis Scott, Lil Wayne, Diddy, Kash Doll, and Nipsey Hussle. The album received generally positive reviews with Fred Thomas of AllMusic praising the album, stating, “The production is clean and engaging, with polished beats and the occasional glossy hook providing a contrast for Big Sean’s visceral rhymes and urgently delivered performances”.
  • In July 2022, Sean and Ellie Goulding teamed up for a new song titled Easy Lover.
