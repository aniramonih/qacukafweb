FAQs About Bret Ernst

How did he get into stand-up comedy?

His transition into stand-up comedy was influenced by his time playing football at university, where his knack for entertaining his teammates in the locker room sparked his interest.

His move to Miami, where he learned about open mic nights at comedy clubs, and an encouraging letter from a friend in New York, pushed him to pursue comedy professionally.

What are some of his career highlights?

He is known for his groundbreaking one-hour comedy special Principal’s Office, the first self-distributed, online special by a stand-up comedian, and his follow-up special Domesticated Animal.

He’s been listed as one of the Top 5 Comics to Watch by Variety and has been featured in numerous top clubs across America.

What makes a comic successful according to him?

For him, the measure of a comic’s success is their ability to sell tickets and sustain a career in stand-up comedy, which he sees as a blend of business and art.

Being on a popular show can significantly boost a comedian’s profile, as it did for him with Cobra Kai, bringing in diverse audiences.

What’s his philosophy on comedy?

He believes that comedy often stems from tragic or dark places, serving as a form of healing for both the comedian and the audience. His personal approach to stand-up involves catching the audience off guard with humorous takes on otherwise not-so-funny topics.

How does he feel about performing stand-up?

Despite the challenges and uncertainties of a career in comedy, he is driven by his passion for stand-up. He has experienced low points, such as living out of his car, but his love for comedy and support from his wife have kept him motivated to persevere.

What roles has he played on TV and film?

He has acted in several television shows and films, including a recurring role on Weeds, appearances on CSI: NY, and a part in Beer League. He also played Cousin Louie LaRusso in Cobra Kai and participated in Vince Vaughn’s Wild West Comedy Show.

What are some of his notable comedy specials and appearances?

He has released specials like Domesticated Animal and Principal’s Office, and appeared on Comedy Central Presents and The Late Late Show.

He has also been featured in Comics Without Borders and had a storytelling spot on This Is Not Happening: Bloodline.

Who is he married to?

He is married to Kristin Nicole Kandrac, an actress. The couple tied the knot on September 21, 2013, and enjoyed a honeymoon on a Caribbean cruise.

What is his background?

Born on September 18, 1980, in Princeton, New Jersey, and raised in Passaic, New Jersey, he has a mixed German and Italian heritage. He graduated from Plantation High School in Florida and later attended Long Island University.
