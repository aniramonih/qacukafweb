Aside from his voice, Freddie Mercury teeth were one of the distinctive reasons he was known.

You could say that he was destined to be extraordinary from birth. The lead singer of Queen was not your typical performer, down to his teeth. 

There are many things to criticize about Freddie Mercury, who left behind a legacy in hard rock influenced by his background and infused with personality. 

But we’ll mainly talk about Freddie Mercury teeth and how they affected his performance and career.

The most intriguing question, why Freddie Mercury Teeth were never fixed, is also touched upon. 

Many contend that all four Freddie Mercury teeth were on the top jaw and pushed his front teeth outward, creating a noticeable overbite. 

Folklore about Queen is that Freddie was afraid that having the extra teeth removed or trying to have the overbite repaired may alter the quality and range of his voice.

What’s Up with Freddie Mercury Teeth?

Freddie Mercury teeth were a very distinctive feature of his personality because of how noticeable his protruding teeth were. 

Freddie Mercury has four mesiodens in his upper jaw, often known as supernumerary teeth. 

Due to the overcrowding brought on by these extra incisors, his front teeth were forced forward and developed an overjet. 

His protruding teeth were caused by a mesiodens disease, which is relatively uncommon. 

It means “extra teeth,” and Freddie Mercury suffered from this problem with his teeth. 

His mouth became crowded with these extra teeth, which forced his upper teeth forward and outward.

His front teeth were significantly larger than moderate, which emphasized this condition. They only replaced his teeth and made his protrusion the center of attention.

In actuality, Freddie Mercury’s teeth received significantly more attention than the rest of his features because of his extra teeth, the crowding, and the overbite. 

His teeth showed out noticeably, and whenever he smiled, it appeared as though they were engulfing most of his face.

How Did Mercury Feel About His Teeth?

You would assume that Freddie wasn’t bothered by his teeth, which is why he never had them corrected. 

However, numerous sources tell us that Freddie Mercury struggled with self-esteem because of his teeth.

In fact, not many people are aware that Queen’s lead guitarist Brian May nearly turned Freddie down because of his teeth. 

The band was concerned about how Freddie’s teeth would impact their reputation and appeal to young people. 

But the future lead singer of Queen won the band’s support by telling him that it gave his singing more fantastic range.

The state of Freddie Mercury’s teeth continued to burden him emotionally, and the lead singer was never at all pleased with his overbite.

According to rumors, the celebrity would try to hide his teeth with his upper lip. At times, he even covered his teeth by slightly holding the microphone over his mouth.

Since Freddie Mercury’s teeth drew a lot of bullying during his school years, his concerns may have started early. 

When Farukh Bulsara’s classmates called him “Bucky,” they teased him.

Ironically, none of the emotional suffering caused by Freddie Mercury’s teeth could convince him to get braces.

Why Didn’t Freddie Mercury Fix His Teeth?

Freddie Mercury teeth were never fixed, despite the fact that aesthetic dentistry was relatively common at the time. 

Freddie has the resources necessary to have his teeth fixed as a lead singer of a famous band.

Freddie Mercury thought his overbite and other teeth were the sources of his singing talent. His four-octave range, which can switch between an operatic melody and a snarl, was a blue gem. 

Few vocalists had these abilities, and Freddie Mercury said his teeth were a big part of his musical career’s success.

Freddie thought that having more teeth offered him more room for career success. 

Consequently, his voices have a more comprehensive range. In other words, the musician sacrificed his sense of identity for his music.

Rami Malek Bought Fake Teeth To Play Freddie

Rami Malek purchased a set of fake front teeth after discovering he was to play Freddie Mercury in the Queen film Bohemian Rhapsody.

The actor revealed at the movie’s New York premiere, ‘I requested to have the teeth approximately a year prior to production, even though I didn’t know if we were going to do the movie or not. Getting used to it took some time. Since it was difficult to converse with them, let alone sing with them, I tried to make myself as comfortable as possible.’

The Emmy winner and Golden Globe nominee experienced some anxiety while filming the movie.

The movie charts the growth of Queen and delves into the life of the band’s late lead singer, who passed away at the age of 45 in 1991 from issues related to AIDS.

The project I undertook was the scariest of my life. How am I going to fill his shoes was my first thought. 

‘He was a guy who was revered as a divinity and a legend.’ Malek acknowledged, ‘He’s a rock god. But I was aware that I couldn’t refuse this. It’s been the most fulfilling and rewarding experience since I went in and gave it my all.’

Final Verdict

Mercury frequently covered his prominent upper teeth with his lip, hand, and subsequently a mustache because he felt self-conscious about them. 

Freddy was never prepared to have his teeth corrected, though. 

 Freddie Mercury refused to fix his alignment problem even though he could have afforded it later in his career because he thought it contributed to his extraordinary range. 

He worried that altering his teeth might make it harder for him to sing.
