What is the Best Currency to Bring to Tahiti?

When planning a trip to Tahiti, one important consideration is the currency you should bring with you. The official currency of French Polynesia is the French Pacific Franc (CFP), which is abbreviated as XPF. This currency is widely accepted throughout Tahiti, making it the best choice for your expenses during your stay.

Using the local currency, the French Pacific Franc, will ensure that you have the most convenient and cost-effective experience while in Tahiti. While some establishments may accept major foreign currencies such as the US dollar or the Euro, you may encounter unfavorable exchange rates and additional fees. It is recommended to exchange your money into CFP before you travel to Tahiti or upon arrival at the airport.

FAQs about Currency in Tahiti

1. Can I use US dollars or Euros in Tahiti?

While some places may accept US dollars or Euros, it is recommended to use the local currency, the French Pacific Franc (CFP), for a more convenient and cost-effective experience.

2. Where can I exchange my currency into CFP?

Currency exchange services are available at Tahiti’s international airport, as well as at banks and authorized exchange offices throughout the island. It is advisable to compare the exchange rates and fees offered by different providers to get the best value for your money.

3. Are credit cards widely accepted in Tahiti?

Credit cards, especially Visa and Mastercard, are widely accepted in hotels, restaurants, and larger establishments in Tahiti. However, it is recommended to carry some cash for smaller businesses and local markets that may not accept cards.

4. Should I bring traveler’s checks?

Traveler’s checks are not commonly used in Tahiti, and it may be difficult to find places that accept them. It is best to rely on a combination of cash and credit cards for your expenses while in Tahiti.

5. Are there ATMs available in Tahiti?

Yes, there are ATMs available in Tahiti, particularly in major towns and cities. However, it is advisable to inform your bank of your travel plans beforehand to avoid any issues with your card.

6. What is the current exchange rate for the French Pacific Franc (CFP)?

The exchange rate for the French Pacific Franc (CFP) varies and can fluctuate. It is best to check the current rates before your trip or upon arrival in Tahiti.

7. Can I exchange my leftover CFP back to my home currency?

Yes, you can exchange your leftover CFP back to your home currency at the airport or authorized exchange offices. Keep in mind that fees may apply, so it is advisable to spend your remaining CFP before leaving Tahiti.

8. Should I tip in Tahiti?

Tipping is not expected or common in Tahiti, as a service charge is usually included in the bill. However, if you receive exceptional service, a small tip is appreciated.

9. What about taxes in Tahiti?

A value-added tax (VAT) of 5% is included in most prices in Tahiti. There are no additional departure taxes for travelers leaving the island.

10. Can I withdraw cash using my credit card?

Yes, you can withdraw cash from ATMs using your credit card. However, be aware that your credit card provider may charge additional fees for cash withdrawals.

11. Are there any restrictions on bringing cash into Tahiti?

There are no restrictions on bringing cash into Tahiti. However, it is always advisable to declare any large sums of money upon arrival to avoid any complications.

12. Can I use mobile payment apps in Tahiti?

Mobile payment apps such as Apple Pay and Google Pay are not widely accepted in Tahiti. It is best to rely on cash and credit cards for your transactions.
