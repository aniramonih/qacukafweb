Jeremy Strong is an American actor who burst into the spotlight after he began portraying the role of Kendall Roy in the HBO television series Succession in 2018, his stellar performance having earned him accolades like the Primetime Emmy Award for “Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series” in 2020. He has also appeared in numerous films including Armageddon Time (2022). He has performed in a number of plays as well, such as his Broadway debut as Richard Rich in the 2008 revival of A Man for All Seasons. Strong was named one of the “100 most influential people in the world” by Time magazine in 2022.

Education

He went to public schools in Boston’s Jamaica Plain neighborhood until he was 10 years old. His parents then shifted the family to the suburb of Sudbury, in the hope of better schools.

Strong kickstarted his acting career at Yale School of Drama. Later on, he got accepted at Yale University and was granted a scholarship. He had intended study Drama but on his first day in class, he found the professor’s lectures so alienating that he made up his mind to immediately change his major to English.

He also studied at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art in London and the Steppenwolf Theatre Company in Chicago.
